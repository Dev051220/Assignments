A database is an organized collection of structured information or data, typically stored electronically in a computer system.

It's designed to efficiently manage, retrieve, and update data according to predefined schemas and rules.

Databases are widely used in various applications, including websites, business operations, scientific research, and more. They provide a systematic way to store, manage, and access data, enabling users to perform tasks such as querying, updating, and analyzing information efficiently. 

Common types of databases include relational databases, NoSQL databases, and object-oriented databases, each suited for different types of data and use cases.
